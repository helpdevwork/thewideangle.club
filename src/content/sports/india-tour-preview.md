Every series preview leads with the top order. This one won't — because the last three away series
in these conditions were decided somewhere else on the scorecard entirely.

## Match-up 1: Pace vs. the middle order

The middle order's record against genuine 140kmph+ pace on bouncy pitches is the single biggest
swing factor in this series. If it holds, India wins the series. If it doesn't, expect a repeat of
the last away tour's collapse pattern.

## Match-up 2: Spin's fourth-innings role

Historically, the team that controls the fourth-innings run rate with spin wins the close matches.
This series has two spinners in form on both sides — expect this battle to be the one that decides
at least one Test outright.

## Match-up 3: Fielding under fatigue

Five Tests in seven weeks is a brutal schedule. Catches dropped in the back half of long sessions
have decided at least two of the last four series between these sides. Watch the slip cordon in
overs 60+ on day three.

## The prediction

If the middle order holds and the fielding stays sharp into the fourth day, India take the series
3-2. If either breaks down, it flips to a 2-3 away loss.
