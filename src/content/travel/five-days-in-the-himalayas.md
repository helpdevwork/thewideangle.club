Five days is enough time in the Himalayas to slow down properly — not enough to see everything, but
exactly enough to stop trying to.

## Day-by-day

**Day 1 — Arrival + acclimatize.** Don't schedule anything ambitious. Walk the town, eat, sleep
early. Altitude sickness ruins more Himalaya trips than bad weather does.

**Day 2–3 — The valley trek.** A moderate 10–12km round trip trail gets you above the tree line by
mid-morning — worth setting an alarm for. This is the shot every gallery on this trip is built
around: cold light, no haze, the whole range visible.

**Day 4 — Village day.** Slower pace, local homestay, and the best food of the trip is almost never
at the "recommended" restaurant — ask your homestay host where they'd eat.

**Day 5 — Departure with a buffer.** Mountain roads run on their own schedule; build in slack.

## What it actually costs

Budget travelers can comfortably do this for ₹6,000–9,000 per person excluding transport to the
region, staying in homestays and eating local. Add a driver/guide for the trek and it climbs to
₹12,000–15,000 — still well under what a "packaged" version of this same trip is sold for.
