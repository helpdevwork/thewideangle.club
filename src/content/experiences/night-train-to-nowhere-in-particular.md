The booking was almost an accident — a sleeper berth on a train leaving that evening, to a station
eleven hours away chosen mostly because it had an available seat. No itinerary. No hotel booked at
the other end.

## What actually happens on an overnight sleeper

Nothing dramatic, which is the entire point. A shared compartment with five strangers who become,
briefly, the only people who matter — a retired schoolteacher two berths over shares food without
being asked, a family traveling for a wedding explains the route better than any map, and the
conversation drifts for hours in a way that a flight's ninety minutes never allows.

## The unglamorous parts, honestly

The berths are hard, the fan above the middle berth is either too loud or broken, and the toilets
are exactly as bad as their reputation suggests. None of this is being romanticized away — it's
part of what makes the arrival feel earned rather than merely transported.

## Why this is the real version of "seeing India"

A flight compresses a country into an airport-to-airport transaction. A sleeper train stretches it
back out into the scale it actually exists at — the several hours between two points where most of
a country's actual life happens, at the speed it happens at.
